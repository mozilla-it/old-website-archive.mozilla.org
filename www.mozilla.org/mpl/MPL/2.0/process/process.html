Process and Schedule

We intend for the MPL update process to follow the basic schedule laid out below. Like any Mozilla release, we ship when it is done, so all of these dates are advisory and subject to change if we need more time.

Process

We intend for the process to center around a series of drafts and rationales. After the publication of each draft and the associated rationale document, we’ll allow time for the community to discuss and comment on the draft through the commenting tool. Contributors who have expressed an interest in actual drafting, as well as Mozilla’s legal team, will then work on the next draft for circulation. Ultimately, Mitchell owns the process and the substance of the license.

Schedule

Mar. 10th: publication of schedule, proposed scope, and FAQ.

Along with this process document, we’ve published a document which discusses our proposed scope.

This Month:

  • Public feedback on text of MPL v 1.1: During this period, if you think you have a problem with the text of MPL v 1.1, you can add your comments to a draft of MPL 1.1 through co-ment (our comment tool).
  • Feedback on scope and process: if you have comments that aren’t appropriate for the comment tool can be discussed during this period on the mpl-update mailing list. Examples of such subjects might include questions about timing and process, or issues whose solutions would require license changes across multiple clauses or paragraphs.

Intermediate Revisions

After the initial announcement, we decided to publish some revisions of particular sections, rather than complete drafts. You can see a list of these at mpl.co-ment.com, and can follow new revisions at our announcements page and on our mailing list.

Alpha 1: Publish the First Public Draft

We will collect the revisions sections into one draft and publish it to mpl-update and co-ment, as well as to other appropriate places. We expect that this draft will provide the baseline for future revisions, addressing easy cleanups as well as some more substantive changes. It will likely not be ‘feature complete’, as we expect that we will not be ready to address all major issues in this timeframe.

Public Feedback Period

During this period, we’ll collect public feedback on the first draft via co-ment and mpl-update.

Beta 1: Publish Second Public Draft

We will publish a second draft to co-ment and mpl-update, as with the first draft. We intend for this draft to address all substantive questions raised in the scope document.

Public Feedback Period

We’ll collect public feedback on the second draft, via the same tools as previous drafts.

Beta 2: Publish Third Public Draft

Public Feedback Period

As with previous drafts.

Publish Release Candidate

We intend for this to have the same language as the final license, but will continue to accept feedback and discussion as with prior releases.

During this time, we will work with FSF and OSI to ensure that the final language is compliant with the requirements of the Free Software and Open Source Definitions.

Fall 2010: publish final license

At this time, we will also publish a guide for updating projects/licenses to the new license.
