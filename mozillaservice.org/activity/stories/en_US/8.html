Community Corridor Planning

By Mary Ann Sclafani Somerville Community Corporation (SCC) is in need of a skilled web designer to prepare a content management system (CMS) for an online mapping tool. As part of our efforts to facilitate a community based planning process around the pending arrival of the Green Line in Somerville neighborhoods, SCC is creating an online mapping tool that utilizes Google Maps to identify community assets and articulate community visions for the areas around each new station. Given the degree of change that will transpire with the arrival of this new line of public transportation, SCC is committed to working with community members, particularly low income families and immigrant residents who ordinarily don’t have a voice, to influence the kinds of changes that will take place. While many of the impending changes will benefit the community, if unchecked, market changes could lead to the displacement of a sector of the population unable to keep up with increasing costs associated with the arrival of the Green Line. SCC is excited to introduce this innovative mapping tool to give people an accessible, unique, and interactive means of understanding and directly influencing community planning decisions.

The CMS will need to support photos, video, and blogging generated by users of the tool. Our intended use of this tool would require that user generated content could be uploaded in real time without administrative discretion but could be altered at a later time for quality control.

Living at Home - livinghome.org

By andrewl I first learned of Living at Home from my mother's many volunteer efforts with the program. The organization enables seniors to remain living in the home of their choice. They link seniors up with programs and services that already exist to serve them, they build wheelchair ramps, deliver Meals-On-Wheels, provide medical transportation, respite care and much more.

Living at Home clearly improves the lives of the people they serve. They have need for financial support and are always looking for volunteers. I have committed myself to spend time during the Mozilla Service Week helping to tell the story of Living at Home by improving their website. The more people know about this wonderful organization, the more they will be eager to volunteer hours or contribute financially to keep the organization strong. Related Link

One Nurse At A Time

By Staci We are a non profit organization created by nurses who are passionate about giving back to our local and global community through volunteer and humanitarian medical pursuits. We are dedicated to assisting other nurses enhance our profession as they too, look for opportunities to serve locally, nationally and internationally.

Our website is an on line resource center for nurses to learn more about volunteer opportunities and to receive support. Our primary goal is to lower the entry barriers for nurses that want to volunteer their time and skills. We are doing this in a variety of ways. We have created a free directory of 250+ organizations that use volunteer nurses, both locally and internationally. We also have a scholarship program where we have been able to provide funds for a volunteer nurse to travel on a trip.

Our technical need is with our directory/database. We need a complete overhaul of the database so it's easier to update (behind the scenes) and faster to load (what the user sees). We would also like an interface between a normal Excel spreadsheet and the website database that actually works so it's not inputting each and every new entry. We are open to purchasing new software if necessary and if it fits within our budget.

We believe that nurses posses important skills and knowledge and are invaluable volunteers locally and abroad. Please help us help those who want to make a difference and change the world One Nurse At A Time! Related Link

Train Us so We Can Give More Youth Futures in Biotechnology

By Sakura Lee We are small organization of 6 staff that has helped over 900 low-income and disadvantaged youth discover science and find opportunities for a future in Biotechnology. In comparison to the state high school graduation rate (as low as 30% for some populations)100% of our students graduate from high school. Most of our graduates pursue a 4 year college degree, many in science. Others choose to continue in our community college and internship program and be later be placed in full-time science-based jobs with wages between $35k-$45k. Our award-winning program has become a model for connecting education to careers, changing the lives of families and building an educated and trained workforce in the growing and competitive biotech industry.

But, we could be reaching more youth and developing a wider network of opportunities with the industry. A technology review/training will help us understand how we can be more efficient in our communication with students/industry, attracting funding and in recruiting more industry partners. Girl Scouts Seek Web Design Assistance

By Allison
Girl Scouting builds girls of courage, confidence and character who make the world a better place. The Girl Scout Council of the Nation's Capitol, as an organization with a membership of over 80,000, greatly relies on volunteers to help get things done, including volunteer recruitment.
